EMR Analysis

The OncoEMR Exclusion: Why It Can't Win Here

üö´ Strategic Barrier: Texas Oncology

OncoEMR = Flatiron Health = Roche.
iKnowMed = McKesson. These are direct competitors.

  • Adopting OncoEMR would share sensitive utilization data with a competitor
  • Would break the iKnowMed ‚Üí Lynx ‚Üí Unity GPO walled garden
  • Loss of "buy-and-bill" spread optimization ‚Äî economically irrational
  • It's a business-strategic exclusion, not technical

‚ö†Ô∏è Functional Barrier: Stanford & Fred Hutch

OncoEMR is ambulatory-only. No inpatient capabilities whatsoever.

  • Cancer care at AMCs involves ICU, surgery, weeks-long transplant stays
  • Separate oncology EMR = fragmented records = medication reconciliation risk
  • No support for hospital billing (HB) + professional billing (PB) split
  • No native integration with Pathology (Beaker) or Pharmacy (Willow)
  • Would require fragile HL7 interfaces instead of native database unity

Bottom line: OncoEMR excels at workflow efficiency for standalone infusion centers. But it faces insurmountable barriers ‚Äî strategic at Texas Oncology (competitor ownership) and functional at Stanford/Fred Hutch (no inpatient module). The EMR choice is a consequence of business model, not feature comparison.
